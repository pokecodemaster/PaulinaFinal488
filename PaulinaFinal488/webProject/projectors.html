Projectors

The projectors that I have worked on are LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) of different brands. When it is time to replace a bulb, the projector notifies with a message that is displayed on screen when it is in use. Most of the time it is something like: Please replace lamp and reset hours. The table belows shows what I need to replace it.

Things I need
to replace a bulb		 New bulb
Screwdrivers
Ladder

The projectors are set up in the ceiling, so most of the time they need to be taken down, depending on where the bulb is located in the projector. If not, it can be replaced right then and there.

Once I take out the bulb with the screwdrivers, I need to clean the filter and reset the hours to know how many hours the projector has used in the bulb.

One important thing I remember is that the lens should never be touched. The oil from fingertips can damage it. If this happens, the bulb can basically blow up.
