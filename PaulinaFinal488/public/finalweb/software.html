Software


All computers, also called PCs, that are available in classrooms and conference rooms have a software package installed:

  • Windows 10
  • Microsoft Office
  • Adobe Acrobat Reader
  • Adobe Creative Cloud
  • Panopto
  • Zoom

There are also built-in microphones, speakers and high definiton cameras that can be used for Panopto to record lectures or for Zoom for videoconference calls.


You will probably want to use Panopto to record lectures, presentations or meetings. There will probably be times where some of the students will not be able to attend class, or students will want to record class presentations. Panopto is a great option to use for these situations. Just take the following steps:

  1. Open up Panopto icon on desktop.
  2. Use your username and password you use for your faculty email to log on.
  3. Select the course you will want to record your lecture in.
  4. New window will open up. Select video and audio on the drop-down box.
  5. Click on Record button to start recording lecture.
  6. When you finish, click on Stop button to stop recording lecture.
  7. Click on View button to watch both video and audio of lecture.
  8. Click on Share button to share with students.

If you need live communication among students and a guest who is not physically present in the building, you can Zoom for videoconference calls. Just take the following steps.

  1. Open up Zoom icon on desktop.
  2. Use your username and password you use for your faculty email to log on.
  3. Click on Create a meeting button to create one and share the link with the guest.
  4. Click on Video button to select the built-in high definition camera.
  5. Click on Microphone button to activate the built-in microphones.
  6. Once the guest has clicked on link you have sent, you will see video and hear audio if they have activated them.
  7. When finished with videoconference call, click on Exit meeting button.


Note: A.V.Impact installs Windows 10, Microsoft Office, Adobe Acrobat Reader and Adobe Creative Cloud as part of the software package. However, for technical support, please contact the Smith IT Department at 301-240-0002 or helpsupport@rhsmith.umd.edu.
