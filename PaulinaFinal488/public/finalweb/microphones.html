Microphones & Speakers

The classrooms are equipped with three types of microphones: wireless lavalier, wireless handheld, wired handheld and receivers. However, in order to use the wireless lavalier and handheld microphones, an external receiver needs to be used. This is also availabe in every classroom and conference room. The table below shows what equipment is needed to make them work with the built-in speakers that are in all classrooms and conference rooms.


Wireless Lavalier Receiver
2 Antennas
XLR cable
Power cord
Wireless Handheld Receiver
2 Antennas
XLR cable
Power cord
Wired Handheld XLR cable

How to make wireless handheld and lavalier microphones work:

  1. Connect antennas to the back of the receiver.
  2. Connect one end of XLR cable to the back of the receiver and the other to the A.V. system.
  3. Connect power cord to power it on.
  4. Power on microphone to use.
  5. To set frequency, press Sync on receiver.
  6. Hold microphone 6 inches away from receiver.
  7. Receiver will notify successful syncing. Test microphone.
  8. Press Voice Audio on touch panel to control volume of microphone.

How to make wired handheld microphone work:

  1. Connect one end of XLR cable to the microphone and the other to the A.V. system.
  2. Power on microphone.
  3. Test it.

All rooms are equipped with built-in speakers. You will be able to hear your voice all over the classroom and conference room.
