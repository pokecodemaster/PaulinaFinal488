Answers

Q. What are your hours of operation?

A. We are open Monday through Friday from 7:00 a.m. to 10:30 p.m. And Saturdays from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. We are closed on Sundays.


Q. How can I contact you?

A. You can contact us by calling at 301-123-4567, via email helpsupport@avimpact.net, or by filling out the form here. Our customers are important to us. If you contact us via email or by filling out the form, we will work on your request as soon as possible.


Q. Do I need to be either a student, faculty or staff of Smith School of Business?

A. Yes. If you request assistance you must be either a student, faculty or staff. In order for us to know if you are part of the Smith School of Business, you must have a Smith email. Even if you request assistance on the phone, your email will have to be provided.


Q. Do I need to log in in order to use the touch panel?

A. No, you do not need your username and password in order to use the touch panel.


Q. When do I need my username and password?

A. You only need your username and password to log into Panopto, Zoom and the desktop computer.


Q. What should I do if something is not working properly and need immediate assistance?

A. In case of immediate assistance you can contact the Smith IT Department at 301-240-0002, via email helpsupport@rhsmith.umd.edu or visit the office 3520.


Q. Can an IT Coordinator from the Smith IT Department train me on how to use the A.V. system?

A. Smith IT staff are trained to provide only immediate technical support. If you want face-to-face training with a professional technician from A.V.Impact, please request so at least 7 days before the start of a semester. A staff member from A.V.Impact will be happy to help you. Note: You must be either a student, faculty or staff from the Smith School of Business to request technical assistance.


Q. How often is the A.V. system checked for good performance?

A. Our programmers make sure everyday that there are no flaws in the performance of the A.V. system. A technician from A.V.Impact personally checks the A.V. system in every classroom and conference room once a week. Either way, if there is something that is not working properly, it is reported and fixed as soon as possible.
